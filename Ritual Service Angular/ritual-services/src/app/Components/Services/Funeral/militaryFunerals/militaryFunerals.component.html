Military Funeral

Going on the last journey is a complex ritual that requires observance of a lot of rules and subtleties. One of the types of burials are military or military burials. They are a specific ritual, full of an atmosphere of mourning for the deceased and a special celebration. Holding such a ceremony is accompanied by a number of military honors.

How are military burials different from ordinary burials?

As mentioned above, the key difference is military honors - displays of respect on the part of individual servicemen. All honors that take place at military funerals can be divided into several categories:

Honors associated with the expression of grief. This group includes the traditional lowering of the state flag and the declining of military attributes; Associated with an expression of respect for the deceased. We are talking about the appointment of honor guards and escorts, mandatory wearing of ceremonial uniforms by participants of the ritual (for civilians present at the funeral, this rule does not apply, they come in restrained black clothes), fireworks from small arms and artillery, which is accompanied lowering of the coffin, as well as a solemn march of guards and escorts and the allocation of a special place for burial; Honors that emphasize the high social status of the deceased and his inseparable connection with the state. This group includes the obligatory abundant use of military and state symbols in the ritual, in particular, attaching cold steel and award ribbons to the lid of the coffin, covering it with a flag, as well as using a tractor equipped with a cannon carriage instead of the usual hearse. Who can be buried according to a special ritual? It is traditionally believed that military honors at burial are characteristic only of the burial rituals of high-ranking servicemen. In fact, this is a mistake, and the list of people who can go on their last journey in this way is much wider.

Indeed, it includes Heroes of Ukraine, veterans of hostilities, wars and military service, servicemen who died while serving and defending the Motherland. Also, persons who have given most of their lives to military service have the right to be buried according to a military rite. These include those who have served more than two decades, as well as those who have been dismissed after reaching the age limit or due to health problems.

Finally, another group of people accompanied on their last journey with military honors are the deceased, who are not connected with military service, but over the years of life have earned a special status before the Motherland. These are the highest state ranks and citizens with special merits before the country.
